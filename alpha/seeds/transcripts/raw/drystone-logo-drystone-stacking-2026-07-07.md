## The brief

Use the classic technique of drystone stacking (mortarless stone building) as the inspiration for a logo:
a small collection of representative stacked stones in a recognizable, natural context, that also has a
simple embroidery representation (so it reads on a hat, patch, or uniform, not just on screen).

## The direction explored

- **Central motif:** a sturdy stack of irregular drystones as the focal point (the stacking technique
  itself as the mark), no mortar, no keystone, dry-stacked, echoing the `drystone` and `cairn` naming
  family (a cairn is dry-stacked waymarker stones).

- **Natural context:** the stones set in a rolling rural landscape (hill, a solitary tree, a winding path,
  distant field) for a grounded, recognizable setting.

- **Two renderings to hold in parallel:** (1) a clean vector logo (crisp lines, natural/earthy palette);
  (2) an embroidery representation (simplified stitch pattern for the stones/landscape, deep-green canvas),
  demonstrating the mark survives as threadwork and stays distinct when simplified for physical
  application.

- **Badge treatment:** a circular emblem with rugged sans-serif border type (a traditional patch layout
  that works digitally and physically).
