## The thesis so far (settled-as-direction, not yet a plan)

From the adoption-chasm research (T11) — point to the source, do not re-derive it here:

- **Only Signal crossed the chasm** among ~16 surveyed P2P / local-first projects. Crossing needs three
  conditions together: **product parity**, a **non-extractive sustaining org**, and an **inciting
  event** — and an inciting event produces *spikes*, not sustained migration.
- **Fourth bridge: institutional mandate.** Matrix's 25+ government adoptions were top-down. "Worth
  designing for explicitly" — an undone design directive.
- **Embedded-trust corollary.** P2P tools must embed in *existing* trust networks, not expect trust to
  form around the tool.

Source of truth (cite, do not duplicate): `../research/p2p-founder-motivations-adoption.md` (RQ2),
`../SOVEREIGN-COMMONS-DOSSIER.md` §7, `../narrative/long-form.md` (adoption-curve risk).
