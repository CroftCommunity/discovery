# PRD — "Thinking of you" (peer-to-peer presence signal)

date started: 2026-06-26 · status: **stub** (S5) — a new starter use case from the 2026-06-26
open-threads review. Modest by design; a good first concrete use case. Fill the template over time.

- **Problem statement** — _(stub)_ a dead-simple way to tell someone you care about that you are
  thinking of them, peer-to-peer, with no platform in the middle and no purchase required. The review's
  framing: instead of buying a connected bracelet (the "long-distance touch" gadgets), you **touch a spot
  on your phone and it reaches out / signals the other person** — that simple.
- **Users / use case** — _(stub)_ close pairs / small circles (partners, family, close friends); a
  lightweight, low-pressure ambient-affection gesture.
- **Settled shape** — _(stub)_ rides the same substrate as chat (a tenant of a durable group, often a
  2-person group); ephemeral signal, optional durable record by choice. Plays naturally to event-driven
  delivery (couples the iOS push posture, `../platforms/ios.md` / T14).
- **Scope** — _(stub)_ in: send/receive a single "thinking of you" ping between consented peers, with a
  gentle local notification. Out (first cut): rich payloads, groups > a handful, any social-feed surface.
- **Open questions** — _(stub)_ delivery guarantee on iOS (opportunistic — T14); the gesture/affordance;
  consent + rate model (it is an interrupt into someone's attention); whether it is its own pad or a
  built-in of Croft Group.
- **Provenance** — `beta/thinking/raw/open threads review Jun 26 at 8-17 PM.txt` (the use case);
  beta `OPEN-THREADS` T15 / S5; couples T14 + `../platforms/ios.md`.
