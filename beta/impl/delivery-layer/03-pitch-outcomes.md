# Drystone messaging: what you actually get

`A short pitch for people who want things that work, not a philosophy lecture`

---

## The one-line version

Messaging that feels exactly like the apps you already use, and keeps working when the apps you already use would not, because there is no company in the middle that can lose your messages, lock you out, or quietly decide what you see.

---

## Start with the part that matters: it just works

Your phone buzzes when someone messages you. You open the app, the message is there. Group chats sync across your phone and your laptop. Nothing about the day-to-day is weird or slow or a science project. If it asked you to care about how it works, it would have already failed.

That is the bar. Everything below is what you get *for free* underneath that ordinary experience, without paying for it in daily friction.

---

## What you get that today's apps cannot give you

**Your messages are yours, completely, on your own devices.** Today, the real copy of your conversations lives on a company's servers, and you have a window into it. With Drystone, the real copy lives on your phone and laptop. The conversation is genuinely in your hands, not on loan from someone who can change the terms.

**It keeps working when things go wrong.** No internet but everyone's in the same room? Messages still flow, phone to phone, directly. The company that runs your messaging app goes under, gets bought, or changes the rules you agreed to? Your conversations and your contacts do not vanish with it, because they were never trapped there in the first place. You can walk away and take everything with you, your history intact.

**Your two phones and your laptop heal each other.** If your laptop was online for a message your phone missed, your phone quietly catches up from your laptop the next time they are near each other. Your own devices act as each other's backup, so your history stays complete without trusting that backup to anyone else.

**No one is shaping what you see to keep you hooked.** There is no outside party deciding which messages rise to the top to hold your attention against your own interest, because there is no outside party in the position to do it. What you see is your conversations, ordered for you, not farmed for someone else.

**The helpers can be fired without breaking anything.** Some conveniences (waking your phone with a notification, holding a message until you come back online) are done by optional helpers. None of them can read your messages, and any of them can be removed without breaking your ability to talk, participate, or leave. A helper that is genuinely optional is a very different thing from a landlord you cannot evict.

---

## Where it honestly gives you a choice

Some groups will want the most robust setup, where a message you miss is reliably waiting for you later. That is the default, and it behaves like a normal app.

Other groups, say a neighborhood mesh with no reliable internet, may choose a lighter setup that depends on no central holder at all, at the honest cost that a message can be missed if you were away too long. The difference here is the rule we hold to: if a message can be missed, you will always be able to *tell* that you missed it. You are never shown a half-empty conversation dressed up as the whole thing. You get to choose the trade; you are never deceived about which one you are getting.

---

## The honest catch

Once you have read a message, it is yours, and the same is true for everyone you talk to. There is no real "unsend" against someone who already saw it, with Drystone or with any other app that claims one. We would rather tell you that plainly than sell you a delete button that does not really delete.

And the freedom to leave with your history means a stolen or lost device already holds what it already saw. You can cut it off from anything *new*, which is a real and useful protection, but what it already has, it has. We build the controls that actually work (deciding what crosses onto a device, and cutting off a device you no longer trust) rather than pretending to control what cannot be controlled.

---

## Why this is possible now and was not before

The hard parts (keeping messages private, syncing across devices cheaply, connecting phones directly without a company in the middle) are now solved, proven, in-use technology. Drystone's job is not to invent them. It is to put them together so that the ordinary, works-like-you-expect experience and the you-are-not-trapped guarantee are the *same* product, instead of a choice between them.

This is not a thought experiment. The pieces are already running in shipping apps, and the specific way Drystone wires them was tested against the real software it would use, the everyday paths (a message arriving by more than one route without duplicating, your phone catching up after being asleep, two phones talking with no internet) were checked and they work. Where a test showed something would not work the way hoped, the design was changed to match reality rather than the other way around.

You should not have to pick between an app that is pleasant to use and an app that respects you. That is the whole point.
