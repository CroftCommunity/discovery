## What this session set out to do

Design the messaging and delivery layer for Drystone, the layer that sits on top of two settled substrate choices:

- MLS (RFC 9420 / RFC 9750) for group key agreement and message protection.

- iroh (core 1.0, shipped 2026-06-15) for transport, discovery, and the gossip overlay.

The starting frame was a four-style delivery taxonomy (direct dial, MLS-over-gossip, MLS-with-DS/meer, opportunistic/adaptive) and a set of rights constraints from Drystone Part 1 that any design must honor.

---

## The substrate facts we grounded first

Before any design, four substrate questions were checked against primary sources. The grounded results:

- **iroh core 1.0** is stable as of 2026-06-15, with wire-and-API stability for the core surface (Endpoint, Connection, Router, ALPN, QUIC + TLS 1.3, relay, key-based addressing). Hole-punch direct connection succeeds roughly 90% of the time, with stateless encrypted relay fallback.

- **iroh-gossip is a separate pre-1.0 crate.** The current published version is 0.100.0, and as crawled this session its manifest pins `iroh =1.0.0-rc.1`, not iroh 1.0 final. This is an integration-residue item, not just "gossip is pre-1.0."

- **Gossip presence is observable without content.** PlumTree lazily pushes the hash of a message (an "Ihave") to peers before content; a topic-subscribed node learns a message exists before, and independent of, fetching its bytes. But this only holds for nodes that have joined the topic swarm; a node outside the swarm sees nothing.

- **APNs / FCM** cap payloads at 4 KB, are explicitly not end-to-end encrypted (so MLS is what makes riding them safe), and throttle silent/background push aggressively (Apple's own guidance: a few per hour, dynamic, may be dropped entirely). Reliable delivery means a user-facing alert push; the invisible channel is the throttled one.

---

## The core reframing: the four-model taxonomy was cut along the wrong joint

The original four conflated three different questions: the path bytes take, who learns a message exists, and what provides durability when the recipient is offline. "Direct dial" is a path; "DS/meer" is mostly a durability mechanism; "gossip" is both a path and a presence broadcast; "opportunistic" is not a model at all but the runtime policy that selects among the others.

The conversation recut the space into **two independent planes**, where a delivery "model" is a pairing of one choice from each:

- **Plane 1, durability:** where sealed bytes wait for an offline recipient, and how they are pulled.

- **Plane 2, presence/wake:** who learns "a sealed message for X exists" and can poke X.

This made the goal of "leverage gossip and a DS cleanly together" expressible as a specific legal pairing rather than a vague aspiration, and it surfaced presence-signaling as the load-bearing axis for the mobile/push problem.

## The decisions reached, in order

- **Direct dial was mis-specified.** It is not "no offline story." On a local link with no internet it is the most center-free delivery path there is, and local queue-and-retry is real participant-provided durability. Plane 1's floor is therefore "self-hosted durability," with the meer as an optimization over it, not the only thing standing between a user and message loss. (Forced by Part 1 P-Durable-Enablement.)

- **Gossip is a legitimate durability dial, not just a presence signal.** A group may rationally accept weak, eventual, best-effort durability to avoid internet-link resource requirements, or to tilt away from any central store for social-utility reasons. The honesty constraint: a group may accept message *loss* (a group-set dial) but the protocol must not allow *invisible* loss (floored by Part 1 P-Knowable-Truth). Those are two separate dials.

- **Durability sources are non-exclusive.** Swarm and meer can both attempt delivery, first delivery wins, duplicates dropped on the byte-identical MLS ciphertext hash. This is the maximum-robustness cell and it costs nothing in principle terms.

- **A third resource-asymmetry role joins relay and meer: the push-notify node.** Byte-free by design: it learns "X has mail, wake X," fires a content-free push, and holds no sealed bytes. It is doubly removable, foreground app needs no push host at all, and poll-a-meer is an always-present non-push wake path. The device-token-to-EndpointId binding is the one irreducible metadata cost, and it becomes opt-in because polling avoids it.

- **The default deployment must be at parity with a normal messaging app.** Center-free is a value-add beneath a parity experience, not a felt cost. Testable consequence: a backgrounded iPhone under the default deployment behaves indistinguishably from a normal app. This stance is currently absent from Part 1 and is stated explicitly in the design doc.

- **The device pool is a secondary, parallel relationship, not a sub-tier.** Each of a user's devices is independently and equally a leaf in the primary group (MLS flatness preserved). Separately, the user's devices may form their own group to reconcile history. Devices decrypt at the epoch and hold *plaintext*; syncing between a user's own devices moves readable plaintext, not sealed bytes. This serves as a personal replay buffer that extends swarm-only durability, recovered over the high-trust composition edge (Part 1 §2.3).

- **History convergence within the device pool is a continuous dial, not a one-time admission gate.** Full all-group convergence is the default; selective convergence (some scopes, some frequency) is the representable non-default. Irreversibility binds only what already crossed; future convergence stays tunable, which is the real seized-device lever.

- **A lineage check on device admission is ideal precisely because admission feeds an irreversible holder.** Once plaintext crosses into a device it cannot be clawed back, so admission deserves the strongest verifiable control, and cryptographic lineage descent is verifiable where "I invited it" is merely asserted. RFC 9750 explicitly names multi-device-same-user as application policy, and RFC 9420 puts credential validation at the Authentication Service, so this is a designed extension point, not a workaround.

- **Cheap device sync uses range-based set reconciliation (RBSR).** Cost is logarithmic rounds plus difference-proportional data, not "difference only." RBSR's ordering key can be any clock-free monotonic order, so it is compatible with the timestamp-free spine. It is a deployed primitive (Willow, Negentropy/Nostr), not theory. It requires a range-summarizable storage backend (a tree with subtree fingerprints), not a flat log.

- **One ordering structure, reused at both layers.** The §7.3.1 timestamp-free causal fold is primary and clock-free. Governance consumes it and *escalates* on concurrency (fork-not-verdict). The dataplane consumes the same fold and *linearizes* concurrency as a zero-stakes UI service. RBSR partitions on the dataplane linearization. For the display tiebreak between causally-concurrent messages, the resolution is the **content hash of the sealed message** (deterministic, clock-free, already canonical as the dedup key), with the wall-clock timestamp shown only as a label, never as the sort key.

- **D-peer (member-to-member reconciliation) added as a fourth durability/replay source.** Members are already entitled to the group's plaintext, so they may reconcile history directly via the same RBSR primitive, generalizing the device-pool replay buffer to all members. Sound only under hard invariants: reconciles self-verifying author-signed records only (never derived or asserted state); dataplane-only, gated by a governance *read* that confirms the partner is an entitled current leaf (the governance plane is never converged through this channel); and multi-party assertion corroboration measures alignment (coverage, defeats single-member withholding) and never validity (the knife: a record's standing never changes with how many members hold it, or a quorum apex has leaked in, which §2.5 forbids). The one cost the blind roles lack: a member is not blind, so reconciliation leaks sync-pattern metadata between members. Whether D-peer is on by default is a per-scope dial weighing that leak; that default is the one decision left genuinely open. The device pool is the high-trust, intra-lineage special case of D-peer (no metadata leak, lineage-gated admission).

- **Exit is final: you keep the history you hold at departure, and may not pull more afterward.** Eligible D-peer convergence range is current-membership-bounded; a former member has no standing to request history post-departure. This is the §2.4 exit right read correctly (leave *with* what you hold, not a standing claim to request more), and it dissolves the former-member-history-pull attack surface rather than defending it: no per-requester historical-range authorization, no serving-member adjudicating membership claims, no fork-boundary behavior, no leak to a hostile group. Honest consequence: a member offline for part of their tenure who left before catching up has permanently lost what they never pulled; this is acceptable because the in-membership replay paths had the whole of membership to fill gaps, which is what makes exit finality fair and gives D-peer its sharpest purpose. Requires a clarification back to Part 1 §2.4.

- **Whole-author absence is an explicit non-goal.** The signed monotonic index makes *provable* per-author gaps visible (you hold index 13 and 15, so 14 is missing). It does not make *whole-author absence* visible (you never heard from B at all), because absence of evidence has no anchor to detect against and is indistinguishable, from the dataplane, between "never sent," "withheld," and "partitioned." Chasing it would burn cycles on what is usually a local reachability condition. The UI may legibly *show* "no messages from B" (honest, cheap), but the system does not hunt for B's possibly-nonexistent history. Missing *governance* acts are covered separately by the (G,D) cursor's G half.

- **Payload classes the selector distinguishes (delivery speed and durability are independent).** Live-durable: fast real-time path *and* full signed durable record (real-time chat done provenance-first, the deliberate inverse of Delta Chat's ephemeral realtime). Intrinsic-ephemeral: non-utterance moment-state (typing indicators, cursors), no durability, no wake, loss is correct; carries a battery/resource default that scales with group size (typing indicators default off above ~7 to 10, tunable). Chosen-ephemeral: a retention disposition on a durable message, cooperative non-retention only, never enforced or corroborable. Self-destruct: a distinct open thread, see below.

- **Two mutually-exclusive dataplane history modes (new companion doc).** Forward-only (large groups; append-only hash-linked fold; cleanup is a coordinated hash roll-up, a separate governance item). Willow-mutable (bounded groups; path-addressed entries with convergent, capability-gated prefix-pruning deletion). Being Willow-shaped already (RBSR, content addressing) makes the mutable mode an evolution rather than a rewrite, at no cost to the forward-only case, a deliberate option-value hedge. Willow prefix pruning is a real coordinated-deletion capability gain but moves none of the trust wall: convergent tombstone among honest nodes, never enforced or corroborable erasure.

- **Self-destruct (open thread, sensitive-value goal): strength is bounded by node fidelity, not cryptography.** It is the inverse of provenance, deliberately minimizing durability and replication (opts out of D-meer and D-peer so no blind store holds the sealed value past its window). The fidelity boundary is the membership and must be legible to the sender, who makes a social-utility judgment about whether the nodes in play are trustworthy enough for the value's sensitivity. Mask-or-remove honored by honest nodes, never enforced. Achievable semantics differ by history mode (real convergent removal under Willow-mutable; display-mask-only under forward-only, where the sealed record stays in the fold). Framed, not specified; needs dedicated investigation. Motivating case: a guest wifi password shown until next Monday, where a good-enough honest means beats SMS-forever or a lost sticky note.

## Validation (experiments executed)

The validation suite ran against live libraries (iroh 1.0.1, iroh-gossip 0.101.0, iroh-base 1.0.1, mls-rs 0.55.2): **13 experiments, 12 CONFIRMED, 1 FALSIFIED.** Folded into design doc §11.

- **The one falsification, E1.1, reshaped a branch, narrowly.** Stock iroh-gossip exposes no application-visible presence signal (`api::Event` is `NeighborUp` / `NeighborDown` / `Received` / `Lagged`; the PlumTree IHave is internal). But this does **not** disable P-gossip: a fabric carrier receives the sealed blob via `Received`, treats arrival as the signal, fires a bare wake, and discards the blob, working on stock gossip with no extra channel. A companion announce channel is explicitly **rejected** (it would reintroduce a TOCTOU race, the LBYL-vs-EAFP hazard, plus a new metadata surface, to save only bandwidth, the least valuable property). The only real surviving constraint is standalone-D-swarm hole detection, which must use payload-embedded signed-index metadata, since detecting what you *missed* cannot ride an arrival signal. Design doc (§4, §7.5, §8.0) and the technical pitch were corrected.

- **Cleared residue:** the iroh-gossip/iroh version skew (E0.1, builds against stable 1.0.1) and the Ed25519 curve (E0.2). 

- **Confirmed the spine:** no gossip replay for offline nodes (E1.2, so meer/device-pool/D-peer replay is mandatory); byte-identical cross-path dedup for one-seal-relayed-twice (E2.1); local-link delivery with relay disabled (E2.2); RBSR cost scales with difference not history (E3.1); clock-free ordering converges identically with content-hash tiebreak (E3.2); push removability and the no-ciphertext-in-payload guard (E4.1, E4.2); selector exactly-once under all path-survival combinations and backgrounded-phone parity (E5.1, E5.2).

- **Demonstrated in model form, not yet end-to-end:** entitlement enforcement (E3.3, XOR stand-in) and lineage-gated admission (E3.4, hash-chain stand-in) validate the *logic*; re-running against live mls-rs validation hooks is open residue.

- **No new required artifact from E1.1** (corrected from an earlier overstatement): the companion announce-ALPN channel is rejected, not required; P-gossip works via discard-the-blob on stock gossip. The only thing E1.1 makes load-bearing is the payload-embedded signed index, which §7.5 already specifies for gap-aware convergence, so it is shared, not new.

- **P-gossip composes with P-push (detector plus actuator), not demoted.** Now that P-gossip needs no companion channel, it is a general cheap detector (a fabric carrier notices arrival) that hands off to P-push (the actuator that wakes a sleeping device). Default presence paths are connectivity-shaped: P-push for backgrounded mobile (with a meer or fabric carrier as detector, poll-a-meer as fallback), detector-or-poll for online recipients, P-gossip as the natural detector in meer-less swarm-only deployments, P-none as the floor. P-meer and P-gossip are interchangeable detectors where both exist; neither dominates. Folded into design §8.0.

- **Methodology doc created (`08-experiment-methodology.md`).** Establishes a fidelity ladder (real-lib / model-form / static) so a stand-in like XOR-as-MLS can never again be read as validating the real mechanism. Hard rules: never substitute a stand-in for the exact component a claim is about; state the rung in the verdict line; pin and print versions; never assert an API shape from memory; a FALSIFIED result is a first-class success; stand-in results generate follow-up real-lib items and do not retire real-mechanism `[confirm]`s. Carries the round-1 follow-ups (re-run E3.3 and E3.4 at real-lib fidelity against mls-rs).

- **P-gossip corrected (the demotion was overstated).** A swarm-subscribed non-member receives the sealed blob via `Received`, makes no move to decrypt, treats arrival as a content-free signal, fires a bare wake (P-push), and discards the payload. This works on **stock gossip with no companion channel**; it is the cleaner, safer solution and the inefficiency (receiving a blob you only needed to know existed) is by far preferable to a separate announce channel, which would reintroduce a TOCTOU race (separating existence from content across two channels is the LBYL-vs-EAFP hazard; atomic delivery has no such window). So P-gossip is restored as a general option that *composes* with P-push (gossip detects, push actuates); the companion ALPN is at most an optional bandwidth optimization and is explicitly rejected as cure-worse-than-disease. What survives from E1.1 unchanged: D-swarm hole detection cannot use a gossip-exposed hash and must ride the payload-embedded signed index.

- **Gap-aware history convergence: one unified mechanism.** Detection and three fill-relationships (D-swarm hole-detection, D-peer member sync, device-pool sync) are the *same* operation: detect a nameable gap, fill it from a self-verifying source. Detection is the **per-author high-water mark**, which *is* the dataplane (D) half of the (G,D) cursor (the cursor and the hole-detector are one object). A single latest signed index from an author defines the complete expected range; any unheld index below it is a nameable gap. A fresh message over any path (swarm, meer, direct) updates the mark, so even gossip's lossy single-message delivery sharpens detection. Whole-author absence stays the explicit non-goal (no mark for an author never heard from). Fill is anti-entropy/RBSR of self-verifying records, priority-ordered by metadata leak: **own device group first** (zero leak, local-capable), then **MLS members** (bounded leak, multi-source to defeat withholding), **never the swarm table** (unentitled). Records are valid regardless of source (self-verification), so priority is driven entirely by leak, not validity.

- **Routing and entitlement are independent populations (the clean decoupling).** The swarm is a routing/delivery population that can be *larger than* and *overlap* the MLS groups running over it; the MLS group is the entitlement population. The sealed message is simultaneously payload (for the entitled), delivery (for the recipient), and gap-definition (for anyone who sees it pass). Reconciliation is a member-plane act: detect a gap from a message arriving over any transport, then do an **MLS member lookup** and request convergence from an entitled member who **need not be a swarm participant** at all. There is no transport gate; the gate is membership. A member reached only via the swarm is still gated on verified membership, not on transport.

- **D-peer participation is voluntary per member (enrollment, not duty).** Acting as a history-convergence sync peer is a service a member opts into, per leaf/member, not a group-imposed obligation. So the scope has a policy for whether D-peer is allowed, and within that, members individually enroll as available sync peers. Fits the cooperative-not-coerced ethos.

- **Provenance doc created (`09-provenance.md`), grounding the upstream lineage.** The delivery mechanism is inherited, not novel: Demers et al. 1987 (epidemic algorithms: rumor mongering is the gossip/D-swarm ancestor, anti-entropy with checksum-until-agreement is the RBSR/convergence ancestor, and the paper already pairs them the way we do); HyParView+PlumTree 2007 (the broadcast-tree refinement iroh-gossip implements); Hyperledger Fabric (closest whole-system prior art for the routing-fabric-larger-than-entitlement-group decoupling, via gossip dissemination plus state reconciliation plus channels-with-anchor-peers). What is genuinely Drystone's: the two-plane decomposition, the MLS entitlement plane over a blind fabric, center-free *ordering and governance* (where Fabric and the literature keep a central ordering service or CA), the rights framing of helper roles, and gap-aware convergence as a named unified mechanism. Honest claim: not a new way to move messages, but a rights-disciplined composition of 40-year-old epidemic dissemination and anti-entropy with modern MLS and iroh, made center-free where prior art kept a center.

- **Naming: established terms for inherited primitives, coinage only for our compositions.** Use *gossip/epidemic dissemination*, *anti-entropy/state reconciliation*, *eventual consistency* (literature terms). Coin only **Delivery Fabric (DF)** for the blind routing overlay and **gap-aware history convergence** for the detect-plus-fill outcome. DS naming settled: "DS" is the upstream MLS Delivery Service role; Drystone keeps its store-and-forward half as the **meer** (refusing the ordering half); there is no separate Drystone "DS." The fabric carries, the meer holds, they are distinct. No collision.

- **Device group reframed: a first-order MLS group, not a special case (§6).** It is an ordinary MLS group distinguished only by scope (lineage-restricted admission, single owner). It moves **sealed PrivateMessage bytes among its leaves exactly as every MLS group does**; leaves decrypt locally because they hold the group key, which is just what membership is. The earlier "plaintext device pool / four-boundaries" framing was dropped as describing the wrong layer: the content moves encrypted, plaintext exists only locally after decryption (true of any member of any group), so there is no plaintext-special handling to fence. Correct description: a **secondary history-convergence backplane with a stronger membership story** (tighter scope, tighter join rule, no new exposure, since every leaf is the same already-entitled owner). The convergence invariants (trust-on-self-verification, dataplane-only-never-governance) hold for the ordinary reason they hold for every group's convergence.

- **Plane model corrected from two planes to three: carriage, durability, presence (§1, §3, §4).** The old model filed "D-swarm" as a durability source and then had to apologize that gossip provides no durability, a category error: the swarm is a *carriage* path (how a message travels), not a durability source (where bytes persist for an offline recipient). Split into three independent planes with one-letter prefixes: **Carriage (C-)** = C-direct, C-swarm, C-relay; **Durability (D-)** = D-self, D-meer, D-peer; **Presence (P-)** = P-none, P-gossip, P-meer, P-push. The planes are paired freely, except where one mechanism serves two by construction, and the doc now flags those fusions explicitly: D-self is also C-direct (one act carries and persists), and the meer is one node on three planes (persists as D-meer, is carry-fetched from, pokes as P-meer). C-swarm is fused with nothing durable, which is now stated cleanly rather than apologized for. D-swarm renamed to C-swarm throughout the design doc and technical pitch; prior-session records retain the historical name. The prefix scheme is defined in the Terms section.

- **Device group is fabric-eligible, which upgrades the durability story (§6.5).** Because it is a first-order MLS group, it can use the full delivery stack including fabric/swarm sync, not only direct local links. So devices converge asynchronously without ever being co-present, through whichever comes online next. Resulting property: **if any leaf of a user's lineage receives a message, every enrolled device eventually sees it**, bounded honestly by reachability (eventual, gap stays visible via the high-water mark) and voluntary enrollment. Safe for the same reasons as all device-group sync (blind fabric, same-owner entitlement, no widening). Whether it rides the fabric is a §2.3 dial, default on (durability), with direct-link-only as the tightening for minimal sync-pattern exposure. This is the device group as a lineage-scoped durability amplifier, the §6.5 personal-replay-buffer role upgraded from local-link-only to fabric-wide.

- **Round-2 experiments created AND executed (`10-experiments-round2.md` plan; `06-Drystone-Delivery-Experiment-Results-R2.md` results), all 11 CONFIRMED at real-lib fidelity.** Built methodology-bound to target what round 1 did not reach, then run against real iroh-gossip 0.101.0, iroh-base 1.0.1, and mls-rs 0.55.2. Outcomes: **R0.1/R0.2 retired both round-1 model-form debts** (entitlement boundary shown cryptographic via HPKE-bound Welcome, not a policy check; lineage rejection at the real `IdentityProvider` commit-build hook). **R1.1 to R1.3** validated gap-aware convergence on real Ed25519-signed records (high-water mark and gap set correct; fresh record widens range and closes gap; tampered/spoofed/forged records rejected source-agnostically). **R2.1/R2.2** validated the routing-vs-entitlement decoupling (non-member carrier relays the 190-byte sealed blob but cannot decrypt it, confidentiality MLS-layer independent of gossip topology; direct off-fabric path delivers the byte-identical blob, dedup key path-independent). **R3.1/R3.2** validated the device-group backplane (two co-entitled devices from one commit each decrypt; tampered record rejected, fake governance assertion ignored, roster read from own MLS view). **R4.1** validated exit finality and the eligibility-vs-validity separation (real Remove commit closes the gate; pre-removal Ed25519 record still verifies across the epoch transition). **R5.1** strengthened the ordering tiebreak to the real MLS ciphertext hash. No model-form debts remain; the one outstanding ladder item is E3.1's RBSR scaling, which waits on the Part 2 §5 production-construction choice.
