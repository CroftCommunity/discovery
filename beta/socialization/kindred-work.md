# Kindred work — people and organizations around Croft

Purpose: a running glossary of real people and organizations whose work is
kindred to Croft/Drystone — potential collaborators, reviewers, and
supporters. Structurally this is an edge list: each entry is an edge from
Croft to a node across tech, philosophy, social governance, co-op practice,
and adjacent fields, and the edge is labeled with the SHARED CONCERN — the
accurately stated overlap between their public work and ours. Started
2026-07-19; seeded from the co-op messaging research arc and prior threads.

## Procedure (adopted into normal discovery practice)

1. Any session that surfaces a named person or organization with genuinely
   aligned work gets an entry APPENDED AT CAPTURE TIME, same discipline as
   the rest of discovery: if the session ended now, the lead should already
   be saved.
2. Every entry records: field, the shared concern (the "Kindred:" line —
   an accurate, respectful statement of the overlap between their public
   work and a Croft thread, never implying they know of or endorse this
   project), the specific artifact of theirs we engaged, a possible role,
   and links. Accuracy is the respect: describe what they actually argued,
   not what would be convenient for them to have argued.
3. PUBLIC-REPO RULE: this repo is public, so this file is written as an
   influences-and-kindred-work ledger — respectful descriptions of public
   work only. Outreach status, contact strategy, and any correspondence
   notes live outside the public repo, keyed by the slugs here.
4. Entries are leads, not endorsements in either direction: listing someone
   claims only that we read their work with profit.

Categories used below: `kindred theorist` (aligned sibling thinking in a
field), `practitioner-operator` (runs the kind of thing we're building),
`movement infrastructure` (orgs that convene/fund/research the space),
`craft precedent` (made a specific artifact we learned from).
