# Logo: the CROFT.ing wall-to-gate wordmark (brief and direction)

**Status: brief and direction only. NO final rendered asset exists yet.** This mark was developed through a
sequence of generated-image iterations; the images are working sketches, not a deliverable, and further
image generation was cut off before a final was produced. This document preserves the converged design brief
for whoever executes the mark. It is a wordmark direction, distinct from and complementary to the
drystone-stacking badge in `logo-drystone-stacking.md` (a cairn/stacking emblem for embroidery); the two
serve different roles — a wordmark/lockup here, a patch-able badge there.

## The concept: a wall that becomes a word that becomes a verb

A single continuous **drystone wall** runs horizontally and, as it progresses, its stones are shaped to
*spell* **CROFT** in a sturdy bold serif — the letters emergent from the wall, sharing the same joints and
textures, not set on top of it. The wall then runs directly into a **wooden farm gate** (with a leaning
spade). The composite reads **CROFT.ing**:

```
   [ drystone wall ......... C R O F T ][.][| gate: "ing" |]  + leaning spade
     plain stones            emergent      period   burned-in
                             stone letters  stone    bottom panel
```

The pun is the point: **Croft** (the noun — the space) becomes **Crofting** (the verb — the ongoing act of
tending). It matches the Shaker stance that a digital home is not bought and shelved but *practised*, and it
maps cleanly onto the domain: personal plots live at `yourname.croft.ing`.

## The converged direction (from the iteration sequence)

- **Emergent letters, carved relief.** The C-r-o-f-t are rendered as carved stone relief integrated into
  the wall — wall texture and cracks run across and into the letterforms so they read as physically composed
  of the surrounding stone, not overlaid type.
- **Granite-filled letters.** The insides of the letters are filled with a rich mottled darker granite-grey
  texture (dark charcoal with fine speckles), for a stone-inlay look; all outlines stay clean black line
  work overlaid on the fills.
- **Tight composition.** The wall is horizontally compressed: the plain wall to the left of the word is a
  short continuous expanse, and the word sits well to the right so that only a **single vertical column of
  stones** separates the final "t" from the gate post.
- **The stone period.** That separating column is given deliberate space (un-squished — a real structural
  element), and one small stone in it, just right of the letters, is darkened with the same granite texture
  to read as a literal **"."** — a full stop at the end of "Croft".
- **"ing" in the gate.** The gate's bottom panel is widened (taller) and carries a **burned-in / branded
  "ing"** — not too elaborate — so the eye travels wall → CROFT → stone-period → gate-panel "ing" =
  **CROFT.ing**.
- **Style and ground.** Clean, consistent, artisanal ink-sketch line work in the deep-charcoal base tone
  (register-mate to `#2F3539` Deep Schist), on deckled cream artisanal paper; motto **GROW YOUR OWN.**
  centered below the mark.
