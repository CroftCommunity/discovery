# Reference index: socialization (brand, voice, adoption)

Every external source or claim-with-provenance the socialization layer leans on, grouped by type, with
the socialization doc that relies on it, an epistemic tag, and a source-quality marker. This layer is
presentation-register: most of its load-bearing empirical grounding lives in `../philosophy/`,
`../activism/`, and `../fenced/`, and is cited to those layers' source-of-record rather than
re-imported or re-verified here. The layer's own discipline (`brand-and-voice.md`) forbids
quote-and-attribute of UNVERIFIED or dialogue-sourced material outward, so every such item is flagged
for a primary-source pass before outward use.

Marker key:

PRIMARY = the external artifact itself (the paper, the published note, the historical record, the
public-domain quotation, the institutional report).

SECONDARY = reporting or summary standing in for an underlying source not directly pulled.

GROUNDED-ELSEWHERE = the load-bearing external grounding lives in a sibling layer's source-of-record
(`../activism/reference-index.md`, `../philosophy/`, `../fenced/`, `../governance/`, `../drystone-spec/`,
`../cairn/`); cited there, not re-imported or re-verified in this layer.

DIALOGUE-SOURCED = the claim originates in the project's own design conversation and is not yet traced
to an external primary; per `brand-and-voice.md` it MUST NOT be quoted-and-attributed outward until
confirmed against a primary source.

Usage tags carried from `brand-and-voice.md` where the source doc assigns one:

CITE = a real external idea or study; attribute to the source, do not present as our slogan.

CLEARANCE = copyrighted / trademarked third-party material; inspiration only, counsel before any
commercial use.

UNVERIFIED = attribution or wording is not confirmed; paraphrase, do not quote-and-attribute.

## Precedents & analogies

Rural Electric Cooperative (1930s). Private utilities refused to run electric lines to rural farms;
farmers formed cooperatives and built/owned the grid themselves; a federal lending program made it
possible; rural electric cooperatives still own much of that infrastructure today. historical-fact.
PRIMARY (specifics not pulled). CITE. Relied on by: `adoption-tactics-and-the-onboarding-wall.md` (the
"digital electric cooperative" plain-language device). Confirm-before-outward-use: confirm the federal
lending program and the continued-ownership claim against a primary source, and attribute, before any
commercial use.

Cooperative-governance lineage — Ostrom, *Governing the Commons* (1990); the Rochdale one-member-one-vote
lineage; the Steem-to-Hive fork as a live anti-capture case. external-framework / historical-precedent.
Ostrom PRIMARY; the co-op lineage GROUNDED-ELSEWHERE (`../philosophy/peer-standing-and-the-cooperative-form.md`,
`../governance/cooperative-and-governance-prior-art.md`). CITE. Relied on by: `brand-and-voice.md`
(evidence anchors; the "community over capital" pillar and "you can buy the tokens, but you can't buy
the crowd"). Cite the governance/philosophy source-of-record; do not re-derive here.

Local-first software thesis — Ink & Switch, Kleppmann et al. (2019); usable decentralized social at
scale — Kleppmann et al. (CoNEXT 2024) and Failla & Rossetti (2024). peer-reviewed / industry-research.
PRIMARY. CITE. Relied on by: `brand-and-voice.md` (evidence anchors for "standing on research, not
vibes"). Also grounded in `../drystone-spec/` and `../cairn/` for the technical claims.

Motivation crowding-out — Gneezy & Rustichini, "A Fine is a Price" (2000); Dan Ariely on social versus
market norms. peer-reviewed-finding / popular-science. PRIMARY. CITE. Relied on by: `brand-and-voice.md`
("community over capital"; financializing every interaction crowds out intrinsic motivation).

Software release trains (alpha / beta / stable channels; long-term support). external-framework /
industry-pattern. PRIMARY (established practice). Relied on by:
`durable-product-and-honest-composability.md` (the LTS-for-interfaces discipline borrows the
channel-and-cadence model). Used as an analogy, not a cited study.

Geoffrey A. Moore, *Crossing the Chasm* (1991) — the technology adoption lifecycle and the "chasm,"
the gap between early adopters (who buy a vision) and the early majority (who buy a product that fits
their life) that most challengers fall into. external-framework / named-model. PRIMARY. CITE. Relied on
by: `adoption-strategy.md` (the "chasm thesis," the organizing frame of the whole go-to-market
argument) and `adoption-tactics-and-the-onboarding-wall.md` (the onboarding-wall framing). Borrowed as
the doc's spine; attribute to Moore, do not present as our own coinage. The field-specific descriptive
map of why decentralized challengers fail to cross is GROUNDED-ELSEWHERE
(`../fenced/platform-dominance-and-adoption.md`).

Enshittification — Cory Doctorow (November 2022 formulation; American Dialect Society 2023 Word of the
Year) — the platform lifecycle (good to users → shift value to business customers → claw value back →
decline). external-framework / coinage. GROUNDED-ELSEWHERE (source-of-record:
`../fenced/reference-index.md`; the argument is carried in
`../fenced/aggregation-theory-and-the-enshittification-shield.md`). CITE. Relied on by:
`adoption-tactics-and-the-onboarding-wall.md` (the "no enshittification arc" trust-compounds pillar).
Attribute to Doctorow; cite the fenced source-of-record, do not re-argue the lifecycle here.
