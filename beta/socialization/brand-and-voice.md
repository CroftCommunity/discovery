# Brand and voice: how Croft sounds

date: 2026-07-07

status / register: working chapter, brand direction not yet settled (open thread **T4**). This is
the how-it-sounds layer: the messaging pillars, the voice and tone, the taglines and quotable lines,
and the working brand material, gathered into one usable reservoir. It is deliberately honest that
the brand carries competing directions that are not yet reconciled (see "The open drift" below); it
presents the reservoir and the tension, not a finished style guide.

## Overview

Brand is how Croft sounds and what it means when it speaks. It is not the argument for why Croft
should exist (that grounded argument lives in `../philosophy/`, cross-referenced here and not
re-argued), and it is not the product shape (that lives in the croft layer, cross-referenced and not
re-described). This chapter is voice, messaging, and brand material only.

Two things frame everything here.

First, the outward-facing discipline. Marketing copy is hard to retract once published, so every
outward line carries a usage tag:

- **OURS** original Croft framing (or public domain); use freely, attribute to Croft.

- **CITE** a real external idea or study; use as support, attribute to the source, do not present as
  our slogan.

- **CLEARANCE** copyrighted third-party text; treat as inspiration only, do not reproduce verbatim
  in commercial material without legal review.

- **UNVERIFIED** attribution or wording is not confirmed; paraphrase, do not quote-and-attribute.

Second, the brand is not settled. There is a genuine, unreconciled tension in the material between
a warm homegrown "grow your own" direction and a low-fi digital-living-room "small circles" direction,
and the product brand-name itself remains open. That tension is surfaced honestly rather than papered
over.

## Messaging pillars

The pillars are the load-bearing claims the voice returns to. They are OURS unless flagged.

- **Foundational layer, not the whole house.** The honest scope claim: cryptography secures the
  building, but it can never be the reason people actually want to hang out inside. Security is table
  stakes, not the pitch.

- **Convenience is not the enemy of principle.** "Purism builds manifestos; convenience builds
  monopolies." We refuse the false choice and design for the everyday case and the crisis case both.
  "Convenience wins the first mile and the last": design the on-ramp and the ordinary day, not just
  the ideology.

- **You own none of this elsewhere; here you do.** Mainstream social owns your feed, your attention,
  and your data for you and against you. The brand hands those back. This is the cooperative,
  non-extractive thesis in user terms.

- **Community over capital.** "You can buy the tokens, but you can't buy the crowd." Anti-capture in
  one line, drawn from the cooperative-governance lineage. Financializing every interaction crowds
  out the intrinsic motivation that makes community real: "hold the door open because it's the right
  thing, not because there's a fee."

- **The field is being reshaped by how it is ranked.** The pillar that engagement-ranking amplifies
  charged content beyond users' choices and stated preferences is a load-bearing factual claim; it
  traces to the citations in `../philosophy/` and should carry that grounding, not stand as an
  unsupported assertion. Treat as CITE-backed; do not overstate past what the philosophy sources
  support.

- **Custodial, not insurgent.** Croft's character is a *seawall, not the raised fist*. The energy is
  *resistance-as-maintenance* — a standing brace against forces that never go away, not a one-time
  uprising that wins and then departs. The point is not to be the only place; it is to be the place
  that is still here, unchanged in what it owes you, in twenty years. This is the emotional register
  of the whole project: durable custody over insurgent spectacle, and it is why the voice stays calm
  rather than combative. The cooperative structure that makes this character load-bearing (member-owned,
  built to persist rather than to win) is grounded in `../governance/`, cross-referenced here and not
  re-argued.

Civic-spine lines usable as compact framings (OURS): "compute provenance, never utility"; "no right
to remove the rights of others"; "equal in rights, not in capability"; "your data lives with you,
the network reconciles"; "small circles, big worlds".

Evidence anchors for "standing on research, not vibes" copy (all CITE, attribute to source): the
local-first software thesis (Ink and Switch, Kleppmann et al., 2019); usable decentralized social at
scale (Kleppmann et al., CoNEXT 2024; Failla and Rossetti 2024); cooperative governance (Ostrom,
_Governing the Commons_, 1990; the Rochdale one-member-one-vote lineage; the Steem-to-Hive fork as a
live anti-capture case); motivation crowding-out (Gneezy and Rustichini, "A Fine is a Price," 2000;
Dan Ariely on social versus market norms); trust and safety as public infrastructure with
user-chosen middleware moderation (Atlantic Council DFRLab, "Scaling Trust on the Web," 2023). One
public-domain quotable: "A corporation has no soul to damn, and no body to kick" (Thurlow / Poynder,
1844).

## Voice and tone

The voice practices the product's own principle: calm surface, depth on demand, nothing forced.

- **Two speeds, always.** The easy answer stays easy and must never become a manifesto at the front
  door: plain, concrete, warm ("your feeds, your people, a space that is actually yours"). The deep
  answer waits for whoever leans in: the cooperative, non-extractive thesis, available to the
  curious, never mandatory.

- **Homegrown, not manufactured.** Low-fi, candid, made-by-a-friend rather than by a marketing
  board. Warm, human-scale, faintly countercultural. The name itself carries this quietly (a croft
  is a small tended plot; small, yours, tended, not industrial).

- **Two registers for two jobs.** "Grow your own" is an imperative spoken *to* the user (the
  invitation). "Homegrown" is an adjective describing what Croft *is*, spoken *about* the project
  (about page, ethos, the voice describing itself). Both true, different rooms.

- **Punctuation and enthusiasm.** Periods over exclamation points. No hype. Let the content carry the
  weight. IYKYK discovery over over-explanation: letting people find a reference makes it feel
  tapped-in rather than pitched.

- **Loose metaphor, not a locked one.** Croft, plot, garden, pond, and "grow your own" share a warm
  natural neighborhood, but the brand does not enforce rigid single-metaphor consistency. Loose
  kinship reads as a rich world; rigid consistency reads as a gimmick. Watch-item: do not over-tighten
  the metaphor until it turns against itself, and do not let American-colloquial water idioms carry
  weight they cannot bear in translation.

## Taglines and quotable lines

The candidate lines are layers of one funnel, not competitors, and the layering is itself on-thesis
(follow-or-ignore applied to the brand).

- **Identity lockup (with the name):** "Croft. Grow your own." The trailing implied blank is
  deliberate; it opens the "own what?" curiosity gap the brand then answers. Likely the strongest
  static lockup precisely because it cannot rotate yet still carries the invitation.

- **Dynamic invitation:** "Grow your own ___" with the completion rotating through a small, vetted
  set. The variety itself is the message: a tagline that holds many endings demonstrates
  composability rather than describing it. Execution discipline is real here; every completion must
  be genuinely true of the product and genuinely felt as un-owned elsewhere. The moment a completion
  is a stretch, it cheapens the device. Draft pool (not finalized): community, feed, space, rules,
  quiet, fun, world.

- **Default rest state:** "Grow your own community." Community is the truest single completion and
  the emotional center; it is where the variety rests. (Open question, see drift: whether the
  implied-blank "Grow your own." is the stronger universal default.)

- **Easy explainer:** "your feeds, your people, a space that is actually yours." The grandma-gets-it
  line. A punchier alternative for skeptics is "Social without the noise," which names a real pain
  cleanly but is defined by negation and reads slightly more corporate-clever than the homegrown
  voice; a clear explainer, not the soul.

- **Supporting lines with narrower roles:** "A warm, sunny place" (mood, not promise; lives under a
  stronger line). "A cooperative experience" (values-honest for the co-op-minded, reads institutional
  to a general audience; the trust layer, not the hook). "Come on in, the water's fine" (warm
  onboarding-threshold invitation, but very American-colloquial and invokes water where the tagline
  invokes garden; not the globally-portable lead).

- **Closing motto (OURS as deployed):** "Every revolution has a maintenance phase. Croft is the
  maintenance plan." The line the cooperative rationale rests on — the answer to *who is still here in
  year seven, and why* — and the register-mate of the custodial-not-insurgent pillar above. Provenance
  caveat: the *every revolution has a maintenance phase* phrasing is unattributable folk wisdom (tracked
  in **T52**), so deploy it as a Croft motto, not as an attributed quotation; the "Croft is the
  maintenance plan" clause is OURS. The grounded cooperative argument behind it lives in `../governance/`
  and `../philosophy/`, cross-referenced here and not re-argued.

- **Digital-living-room framing (OURS, derived):** the digital living room versus the public
  shouting-square; invite-only, tight-knit; "Small circles, big worlds." / "Find your world." The
  small-tight-group value prop ("sometimes, ten people are the whole world") is an OURS paraphrase
  that sidesteps IP exposure; the specific hit-show line that inspired it is CLEARANCE (inspiration
  only, counsel before any commercial use, and the bigger exposure is trademark / false endorsement,
  not copyright).

## The open drift (T4, not yet reconciled)

The brand carries competing directions that are not settled. Surfacing this is the point of the
chapter; do not read any one of these as final.

- **Voice direction.** Two pulls coexist without a decision. One is the warm, homegrown,
  garden-native "grow your own" world (cream-green-earth, tended, made-not-manufactured). The other
  is a low-fi, lowercase, digital-living-room / IYKYK register aimed at a younger, micro-community-
  native audience. They are compatible in principle and not yet reconciled into one dominant voice.

- **Default tagline form.** Whether the lead is the rotating "Grow your own ___", the rested "Grow
  your own community", or the implied-blank "Grow your own." is open. The rotation set (which small
  final set of completions earns a place) is also unfinalized; the wrong word cheapens it.

- **Product brand-name.** The umbrella name (Croft) and the protocol name (Drystone) are settled, but
  the product brand-name is still open drift and must be reconciled before the brand chapter can
  harden. This is the standing gate on T4.

- **Clearance and provenance gates.** CLEARANCE items (the hit-show line) need counsel before any
  commercial use, and UNVERIFIED anecdotes (for example the door-holding "corporation versus person"
  story, widely retold but not verifiably sourced) must be confirmed or paraphrased before they leave
  the building.

## What this establishes (and does not)

This establishes a single reservoir for how Croft sounds: the messaging pillars, the voice and tone
guidance, the tagline funnel and quotable lines with their usage tags, and the evidence anchors,
gathered so nothing scatters and so the brand tension is visible in one place. It does **not** settle
the brand or voice direction, pick a default tagline form, resolve the product brand-name, or clear
the CLEARANCE and UNVERIFIED items; those remain the open T4 gate. The grounded argument behind these
framings lives in `../philosophy/`, and the product shape lives in the croft layer; this chapter
neither re-argues the one nor re-describes the other.
