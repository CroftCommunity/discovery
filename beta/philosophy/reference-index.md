# Reference index: the philosophy layer ("why it is right")

Every source cited across the philosophy layer, grouped by type, with the doc(s) it grounds, its
epistemic-status tag, a primary/secondary marker, and a resolvable locator where one exists. The
philosophy layer is unusually quotation-heavy and most of its book and essay citations were
surfaced in AI discussion sessions rather than read from a primary edition; those carry the
layer's standing instruction, "confirm against the primary edition before publish," and are marked
accordingly. Do not upgrade a flagged quotation to a clean citation on the strength of this index.

Marker key:

PRIMARY = the artifact was retrieved and the cited content verified this session, or is confirmed
by the project's own fact-check pass.

PRIMARY-VENUE = spoken remarks reported by the venue at which they were made (closest available
primary).

SECONDARY = reporting or summary standing in for an underlying document that is not public.

NOT-YET-PULLED = the work is named and identifiable, but no primary edition was consulted; the
citation, and any quotation drawn from it, is unverified.

Doc short-names used under "grounds": *commensurability* (Commensurability and the Two Ledgers),
*cybernetic-failure* (Cybernetic failure and the variety argument), *epistemics* (Epistemics:
provenance and verification), *lifeworld* (The Lifeworld and the System), *peer-standing* (Peer
Standing, the Securitized Corporation, and the Cooperative Form), *structural-argument* (A
Structural Incompatibility Between Securitized Platforms and Peer Standing), *peer-rights-razor*
(The peer-rights razor and its lineage), *proof-of-personhood* (Proof of personhood and the
identity layering), *recurring-inversion* (The recurring inversion), *modular-politics* (prior-art/Modular Politics analysis).

*peer-rights-razor* ("code is speech"). PRIMARY, qualified. documented-case: the doc notes honestly
