# Reference index: "the enclosure inversion, in land and stone"

Every source drawn on across the `history/` layer, grouped by type, with its locator where known, its epistemic-status tag, a primary/secondary marker, and which history doc relies on it. Unlike the empirical layers, most of this material is named in prose rather than by author-year, and a large share is deliberately carried as flagged folklore or contested figure — those flags are load-bearing and are preserved here exactly as the docs carry them.

The two history docs indexed here are referred to by short label:

- **[crofting]** = *Crofting, dry-stone, and the enclosure inversion* — the deep-history record (the word, the trap, the Clearances, the Crofters' War, the 1886 settlement, John Clare, the arc to the enclosure of connection).
- **[present+global]** = *The enclosure inversion, present and global* — the companion that extends the thesis into the living present (Right to Roam, the 1607→2020 loop, "Scotland won twice"), across civilizations (global dry-stone), and welds it to the architecture (the scholarly spine, "the name is the thesis").

Marker key:

PRIMARY = the artifact itself (the poem or play text, the statute, the sworn/recorded testimony, the archival document, the standing structure).

SECONDARY = a historian, survey, or account standing in for underlying record not quoted directly here.

FOLKLORE = a popular telling or attributed saying carried explicitly as unverified colour, never as settled fact.

NOT-YET-PULLED = the primary is named but its verbatim text sits behind a paywall or was not retrieved; do not quote verbatim until confirmed.

Epistemic tags: documented-historical-fact; primary-text; archival-record; etymological-record; scholarly-framework; contested-figure; popular-telling. Where a doc attaches `[UNVERIFIED]` or `[confirm]`, that flag is reproduced verbatim.

## Books & authors

Hayes, Nick. *The Book of Trespass: Crossing the Lines that Divide Us* (2020). Co-founder of the Right to Roam campaign; the source of the "the fence creates the crime" inversion and the Boughton House / Montagu-line loop. PRIMARY. documented-historical-fact / framing. NOTE: the headline quote is carried as "> The fence creates the crime. — Nick Hayes, *The Book of Trespass* (2020) `[UNVERIFIED, confirm before publish]`". Relied on by [present+global].

Shrubsole, Guy. *Who Owns England?* Co-founder of Right to Roam with Hayes; the survey the land-ownership figures (the ~one-third-of-Britain and related access figures) trace back to. SECONDARY (the survey stands behind the figures; figures themselves carry `[confirm]`). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Thompson, E. P. *Moral economy* (the concept — pre-enclosure common life held by custom and mutual obligation rather than price, so enclosure is the disintegration of a working social order, not an efficiency). The central plank of the scholarly spine under the poems. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. Relied on by [present+global]. NOTE: named by concept in prose; the specific text (e.g. *Customs in Common* / *The Making of the English Working Class*) is not cited by title — NOT-YET-PULLED for a precise locator.

Williams, Raymond. *The Country and the City*. Supplies the frame that the elegy for a lost rural England is itself a recurring literary move, and the honest version names real dispossession rather than pastoral fantasy. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. Relied on by [present+global].

Freeman, Jo. *The Tyranny of Structurelessness* (1972). The intellectual root of the "Princeps Problem": declaring there is no structure does not abolish power, it only hides it. PRIMARY (named essay). scholarly-framework. Relied on by [present+global].

Campbell, John Lorne (historian). Credited with the "Magna Carta of the Highlands and Islands" phrasing for the 1886 Act. SECONDARY. contested-attribution. NOTE: attribution is `[UNVERIFIED]`; two phrasings circulate. Relied on by [crofting].

Chambers, Robert. Quoted approving the substitution of sheep for "innumerable hordes of useless human beings" (Clearances-era). FOLKLORE. NOTE: `[UNVERIFIED]`. Relied on by [crofting].

Marx, Karl. *Capital*, vol. 1 (1867). Gave the enclosure process its lasting scholarly name, *so-called primitive accumulation* — the forcible expropriation of the people from the soil — the theoretical capstone under the enclosure-as-theft reading. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. NOTE: the coined term *so-called primitive accumulation* is carried as a term of art; the verbatim phrase "the systematic theft of communal property" is folded (tranche-2) as a set-off block quote in [crofting] §3, flagged `[UNVERIFIED, confirm against primary edition before publish]` (phrase as cited via *Science for the People*, "The Goose on the Common", not confirmed against the primary edition). Relied on by [crofting] §3.

Devine, T. M. *The Scottish Clearances* (2018); *Clanship to Crofters' War*. The modern documentary account behind [crofting] §2's de-romanticized reading: landlords eliminated the tacksmen to capture their cut, and the new crofts were engineered undersized as a labour-tying arrangement dressed as land reform. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. NOTE: also the source of the contested-genocide caution, folded (tranche-2) into [crofting] §2 as attributed synthesis carrying the verbatim fragments "ludicrous to suggest a genocide" and "no direct evidence of increasing mortality caused by clearances" (Highland population rose 1801–1850s), flagged `[UNVERIFIED, confirm against primary edition before publish]`. Verbatim source is `croft-crofting-research.md` (the honest-counterpoints section), not the etymology transcript; the source renders the two fragments ellipsis-collapsed, so they are carried as separate quoted fragments rather than one contiguous block quote. Relied on by [crofting].

Hunter, James. *The Making of the Crofting Community* (1976). The standard history of how the crofting community was formed out of the Clearances; anchors [crofting] §2's account of crofting as a landlord invention rather than an ancient survival. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. Relied on by [crofting].

Richards, Eric. *Debating the Highland Clearances*. The historiographical survey that argues from the contested parts (scale, villainy, the genocide framing) and supplies the discipline for keeping the Clearances register honest without the cartoon villain. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. Relied on by [crofting].

Hardin, Garrett. *The Tragedy of the Commons* (1968). The canonical modern statement that a shared resource is doomed to overuse — the "communal tenure is inefficient" claim in its purest form. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. NOTE: carried in [crofting] §3 as the position that Ostrom and the enclosure historians refute, not as an endorsed view. Relied on by [crofting].

Ostrom, Elinor. *Governing the Commons* (1990). Refuted Hardin empirically: real common-pool resources (the Törbel alpine commons, stably managed for centuries) can be governed sustainably by their own users without privatization or the state. In [crofting] §3 this is the enclosure-rebuttal register — "the commons weren't overused but stolen"; Ostrom's IAD/governance framework itself is homed in `philosophy/` and `drystone-spec/`, not restated here. SECONDARY. scholarly-framework. Relied on by [crofting].

## Poems & primary texts

Clare, John (1793–1864), agricultural labourer of Helpston; parish enclosed by award 1820. Texts are public domain; spelling/punctuation are editor-dependent. PRIMARY. primary-text. Relied on by [crofting] (all three poems) and [present+global] (*Remembrances*, via the Captain Swing reading).
- *The Mores* — "Inclosure came and trampled on the grave / Of labour's rights and left the poor a slave"; "A board sticks up to notice 'no road here'." [crofting].
- *Remembrances* — mourns specific named places; the hanged figures read plausibly as an allusion to labourers executed after the 1830 Captain Swing risings. [crofting] and [present+global]. FOLDED (tranche-2): the Napoleon-simile / hanged-moles lines ("Inclosure like a Buonaparte let not a thing remain / It levelled every bush and tree and levelled every hill / And hung the moles for traitors – though the brook is running still") are folded as a set-off block quote in [crofting] §4, flagged `[UNVERIFIED, confirm against primary edition before publish]`.
- *To a Fallen Elm* — "And barked of freedom, O I hate that sound / It grows the cant terms of enslaving tools / To wrong another by the name of right." [crofting]. FOLDED (tranche-2): the second, tighter couplet in the same poem — "Self interest saw thee stand in freedoms ways / So thy old shadow must a tyrant be" — is folded as a set-off block quote in [crofting] §4 alongside the passage already carried, flagged `[UNVERIFIED, confirm against primary edition before publish]`.

Ball, John. Couplet from the 1381 Peasants' Revolt: "When Adam delved and Eve span, / Who was then the gentleman?" Ball was hanged, drawn, and quartered. PRIMARY (attributed verse). primary-text / documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Winstanley, Gerrard, and the True Levellers / Diggers. Began cultivating the common at St George's Hill, 1649; declared the aim to make "the Earth a common treasury for all, both rich and poor" (primary text); the colony was broken up within a year. PRIMARY. primary-text. Relied on by [crofting] and [present+global] (as the root of the scholarly spine).

Anonymous goose verse: "The law locks up the man or woman / Who steals the goose from off the common, / But lets the greater felon loose / Who steals the common from the goose." PRIMARY (anonymous protest verse). primary-text. NOTE: dating `[UNVERIFIED]`; often cited mid-to-late eighteenth century, hard attestations 1816 and 1821. Relied on by [crofting] (and echoed in [present+global]).

*Middle English Dictionary*, entry `croft n.`, MED10396 (open-access, directly checkable). The sense-split (working plot / tournament arena / abstract enclosure / hell) that grounds the "croft is a concept, not a definition" claim. PRIMARY. etymological-record. Relied on by [crofting].

*Towneley Plays* (morality plays). "Com to my crofte [hell]" — the sense in which *croft* names hell. PRIMARY. primary-text. Relied on by [crofting].

*Oxford English Dictionary* — the 1772 first attestation of the verb "crofting" (Manchester Directory, Lancashire cloth-bleaching trade). NOTE: the 1772 date is solid; the exact verbatim sentence and the bleaching-versus-farming sense are `[UNVERIFIED]`, behind the OED paywall. NOT-YET-PULLED (verbatim). etymological-record. Relied on by [crofting].

Toponymic / onomastic record: place-names (Stancroft 1175, Oxecroft 1288, Barleycroft 1423) and locative surnames (Hugo de Croft, 1163). PRIMARY (attested record). etymological-record. Relied on by [crofting].

## Acts, commissions & events

Crofters' Holdings (Scotland) Act 1886. Secured tenancy, fair rents, heritability, and protection of the common grazing; pointedly refused to return cleared land. PRIMARY (statute). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting]; referenced by [present+global] as the first of the two Scottish wins.

Napier Commission (report 1884; evidence taken 1883). Preserved verbatim testimony of hundreds of crofters, most in Gaelic. First witness Angus Stewart of Braes (8 May 1883) summarised the remedy as "give us land." PRIMARY (commission testimony). archival-record / documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Sheriff William Ivory report on the Braes crowd — "attacked us furiously first with curses, and then with sticks, stones and other missiles" — National Records of Scotland, GD1/36/1/3/52. PRIMARY. archival-record. Relied on by [crofting].

Bernera Riot (Great Bernera, Outer Hebrides), 1874. The first courtroom vindication that crofters could no longer be quietly driven off the land; precedes the more famous risings by ~a decade. PRIMARY (event/legal record). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Battle of the Braes, 1882, and Glendale, 1883 — the rent strikes and land-raids of the Crofters' War. PRIMARY (events). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2003 — the Scottish right-to-roam; the second of the two Scottish wins ("Scotland won twice"). PRIMARY (statute). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

1607 Newton rising (Northamptonshire). The crowds "levelled" hedges and "dug" up ditches, coining Levellers / Diggers; Sir Edward Montagu was sent to suppress it and rose into the Montagu (Buccleuch) landholding — the near end of the 1607→2020 loop. PRIMARY (event). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting] (the naming) and [present+global] (the loop).

Kett's Rebellion, 1549 — Kett tore down the hedges and was hanged from Norwich Castle. PRIMARY (event). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Peasants' Revolt, 1381 — the setting for John Ball's couplet. PRIMARY (event). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Community-ownership buyouts: Assynt (1993), Eigg (1997) — the "truest modern echo" of the settlement, community holding the commons rather than the hedged individual right-to-buy. PRIMARY (events). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Captain Swing risings and rick-burnings, southern England, 1830 — labourers executed afterward, read into Clare's *Remembrances*. PRIMARY (events). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Right to Roam campaign (present-day) and the Boughton House trespass. The estate is the seat of the Duke of Buccleuch (Montagu line), largest private landowner in Britain; the campaign's mapping of the 1607→2020 loop and the Weekley Hall Wood felling. PRIMARY-AS-REPORTED (contemporary campaign / events). documented-historical-fact, with figures flagged. Relied on by [present+global].
- Access figures: public shut out of ~92% of England's land and ~97% of its rivers `[confirm]`; hereditary aristocrats hold ~a third of Britain `[confirm]`; public footpath network roughly halved, to ~118,000 miles, since the nineteenth century `[confirm]`. contested-figure.
- Firewood sold back at ~£95 a cubic metre `[confirm]`. contested-figure.

Global enclosure parallels (named in prose as the recurring pattern; each is a documented episode standing in as analogy, not independently sourced here): Rome's *ager publicus* and the Gracchi (133 BC); Bengal's Permanent Settlement (1793); Stolypin's breaking of the Russian commune (1906); Mexico's *ejido* (dissolved 1856, restored 1917, re-privatized 1992, triggering the Zapatista rising); the American West (Homestead Act 1862, barbed wire, Dawes Act 1887). SECONDARY. documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting].

Refuted / not carried: the various proposed name-stories the [crofting] doc explicitly refuses, and the Gaelic *dùthchas* hereditary-stake framing (carried as established context, not flagged). One modern land-court case in which a crofter prevailed over a landlord's tree-planting scheme on common grazing is cited but `[UNVERIFIED]`. FOLKLORE / unverified.

## Global material-history cases (dry-stone as a cross-civilizational structural law)

Incan ashlar (Peru). Polyhedral interlocking stones, inward-sloping mortarless walls; Incan foundations stood when Spanish colonial mortared structures collapsed in major earthquakes. PRIMARY (standing structures). documented-historical-fact. NOTE: the vivid "dancing stones" quake mechanism is carried as `[confirm — the dramatic "dancing stones" mechanism is popularized and debated, not rigorously measured]`; the confirmed fact is the survival outcome, not the mechanism. Relied on by [present+global].

Japanese *ishigaki* (castle foundations — Kumamoto, Himeji). Massive curving mortarless foundation walls over a loose gravel-packed core; sloping profile distributes load, loose backing drains groundwater. PRIMARY (standing structures). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Great Zimbabwe. Dry-stone granite walls up to eleven metres high, raised by the ancestral Shona between the 11th and 15th centuries; inward batters and curved layouts; still standing under their own weight. PRIMARY (standing structures). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Mediterranean *trulli* and terracing (Greece, Italy, Spain; Alberobello). Dry-stone terraces as arable land and drainage fields; conical *trulli* roofs corbelled from overlapping flat limestone, no mortar and no keystone. PRIMARY (standing structures). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [present+global].

Dry-stone construction (the base craft the names *Drystone* and *cairn* are taken from): footing boulders, full-width through-stones, and hearting stones doing different work but equal in importance, with no keystone; the cairn as dry-stacked, many-handed, keystone-less waymarker. PRIMARY (material fact). documented-historical-fact. Relied on by [crofting] (the craft) and [present+global] (the global generalization).

## Coverage and gaps

What is solid PRIMARY and safe to stand on: the poem and play texts (Clare, Ball, Winstanley/Diggers, the goose verse, the Towneley line), the statutes and events (1886 Act, 2003 Act, Napier Commission testimony, the Ivory report at NRS GD1/36/1/3/52, Bernera 1874, the Braes/Glendale, 1607 Newton, Kett 1549, the Assynt/Eigg buyouts), the etymological record (MED10396, the toponyms/surnames), and the standing global dry-stone structures. These carry no verification flag in the source docs.

The most notable open gap: the crofting-narrative de-romanticization and the Right to Roam access figures are the softest load-bearing material. The single largest unverified quantitative claim is the pair of headline access figures — the public shut out of **~92% of England's land and ~97% of its rivers** — both carried as `[confirm]`, sourced through Hayes/Shrubsole rather than to a primary land-ownership dataset; the one-third-of-Britain and 118,000-mile-footpath figures share that status. On mechanism, the Incan **"dancing stones"** earthquake explanation is explicitly flagged as popularized and debated (only the survival *outcome* is confirmed). Attribution gaps: the "Magna Carta of the Highlands" credit to John Lorne Campbell is `[UNVERIFIED]`, and the goose verse's dating is `[UNVERIFIED]` (hard attestations only 1816/1821). NOT-YET-PULLED verbatim: the OED 1772 "crofting" sentence (paywalled). Folklore carried as colour, never as fact: the "lairds' four-footed clansmen" epithet, the Chambers "useless human beings" quote, the evicted-elder's curse, and the unnamed modern tree-planting land-court case. The Thompson *moral economy* and Williams *The Country and the City* references are named by concept/title but without page-level locators — adequate for a scholarly-spine citation, insufficient for direct quotation.

Scholarly spine added (tranche-1 recovery): [crofting] now carries the economic-history anchor beneath its enclosure-as-theft argument — Marx's *so-called primitive accumulation* (*Capital* vol. 1, 1867) as the canonical name for the process; Devine / Hunter / Richards as the modern Clearances scholarship behind §2's de-romanticized reading; and the Hardin (1968) vs Ostrom (1990) pairing as the direct rebuttal to the "communal tenure is inefficient" Improvement rhetoric ("the commons weren't overused but stolen"). The Devine contested-genocide caution is folded into §2 as attributed synthesis carrying the verbatim fragments "ludicrous to suggest a genocide" and "no direct evidence of increasing mortality caused by clearances" — kept as separate quoted fragments rather than one contiguous block quote because the verbatim source (`croft-crofting-research.md`, the honest-counterpoints section, not the etymology transcript) renders them ellipsis-collapsed.
