## Headline (read before relying on anything)

**The Missouri Chapter 351 framework is real and accurately described** — the section range
(351.1000–351.1228), Senate Bill 366 / 2011 / effective Aug 28 2011, nonpatron (investor) members,
multi-class voting, sweat-equity contributions, and most cited subsections **CONFIRM**. All tax-side
claims (501(c)(12)/(14), the 85% member-income test, Subchapter T patronage, the 4.0% MO corporate
rate) **CONFIRM**. The platform-coop case studies and the MFA/Hirth history **CONFIRM** — including
Coomappa, which is real (not fabricated). **Do NOT rely on these confirmed-wrong specifics:**

1. **`§ 351.1015 ≠ "powers of a natural person"` — REFUTED.** That section is the **articles-of-
   organization contents** provision. The powers provision is **`§ 351.1036`**. (Affects the
   "can the co-op spend on marketing/sponsorship" answer — the *answer* is yes, the *citation* is wrong.)
2. **Articles (Form CA 41) fee is $100, not $105.** $105 is the LLC fee; likely conflated. Verify the
   current SOS schedule.
3. **Name reservation is $25, not $20** (60-day hold).
4. **Git/Inkscape are Software Freedom Conservancy (SFC) projects, not CS&S.** SPI hosts Debian/Arch/
   PostgreSQL ✓; the claim mis-pairs Git/Inkscape with CS&S.
5. **CHS FY2023 revenue ~$45.6B, not $39B**; **DFA is Kansas City, *Kansas***, not Missouri.

Directionally-right-but-imprecise: "modeled on ULCAA" (plausible, not explicitly stated in primary
sources — **PARTLY**); "§ 351.1111 = supermajority amendments" (it governs member voting thresholds
incl. heightened requirements — **PARTLY**). Diaspora's "PayPal froze the funds" detail
**[UNVERIFIED]**.

  anti-patterns the design explicitly answers — good material for `crystallized/principles.md` (the

  SFC, CS&S; Open Source Collective is a 501(c)(6) (corporate write-off, not individual charity); the
  Open Collective *Foundation* dissolved end-2024. Git/Inkscape belong to **SFC** in any distilled note.
