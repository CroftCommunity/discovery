## Errors not to carry forward

1. **"Skartsia and Tomi" (Greek/Italian dry stone terracing terms) — REFUTED / fabricated.** No
   source links either word to terracing. The real Greek terms are **xerolithia** (ξερολιθιά, the
   dry-stone walling craft) and **pezoules** (πεζούλες, the terraces themselves). Plausible-sounding
   hallucinated Greek. (src: cycladicidentity.gr; samoswineroutes.gr)
2. **"Adelphity" as "an older English word" — PARTLY (framing REFUTED).** The Greek root *adelphos*
   ("brother") and the derivatives *adelphic* / *adelphian* are attested, but "adelphity" is **not in
   OED or Merriam-Webster** — it is a recent inclusive coinage (cf. French *adelphité*, ~2017), not an
   old English word. (src: oed.com; merriam-webster.com)
3. **"Farid" lumped under root W-H-D — PARTLY.** The A-N-S = friendliness/companionship reading
   (Anisa) is correct, but **Farid derives from the distinct root F-R-D** (فرد, "to be unique/alone"),
   **not** W-H-D (the root of Wahid). Two different triliteral roots. (src: behindthename.com;
   en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farid)
4. **"Musha-gaeshi" = the curve / weight-distribution profile — PARTLY (gloss REFUTED).** The curved
   gentle-base/steep-top profile of ishigaki is real, but **musha-gaeshi literally means "warrior
   repelling"** — it names the anti-climbing *defensive effect*, not the engineering load path. The
   physical feature is accurate; the etymology gloss is wrong. (src: visit-kyushu.com)
5. **Stone-craft terms "Builders" and "Packing" — PARTLY / non-standard.** "Builders" is **not** a
   real waller term for face stones (the terms of art are **face / facing stones**); "Packing" is loose
   — the centre fill is **hearting**, and the small wedge stones are **pinning**. The "length into the
   wall" rule and the terms footings / through stones / hearting / dyke / waller / dyker are all
   correct. (src: thestonetrust.org; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dry_stone)

## Why this matters for Croft design

- **The anti-pattern is the live one for this project, and it is real.** "Nominal peer equality
  masking capability asymmetry → de-facto authority" (BitTorrent seeders, PoS/PoW pools) is the exact
  failure Croft's existing principles already guard against: **"all peers are equal in rights, not
  capabilities"**, the **F5 proof** (availability-as-rights-escalation — no governance right escrowed
  to a superpeer's presence), the **meer anti-entrenchment** design (materially-reversible delegation),
  and **"different, not weaker."** The user named this anti-pattern explicitly as the thing to guard
  against — it should anchor the Drystone protocol-name rationale in `NAMING.md` and the
  capability/rights split in `crystallized/principles.md` (Tier 1). Link the two in COHESION.
- **The Drystone metaphor is load-bearing the *right* way.** Its design instruction — *bring
  capability asymmetry into the light and encode it structurally, while locking constitutional rights
  at 1:1* — is congruent with the corpus's "structural, not runtime, enforcement" principle and with
  honest-seams. The metaphor (footings/through-stones/hearting ↔ seed/desktop/mobile) is a
  presentation aid, **not** an implemented routing tier; do not let it harden into a claimed mechanism
  in the protocol spec without a proof. Treat as design-vocabulary, status `design`.
- **Etymology/term hygiene:** if any of these words surface in published docs, use the corrected
  forms — xerolithia/pezoules (not "Skartsia and Tomi"); face/facing stones, hearting, pinning (not
  "builders"/"packing"); attribute the anti-pattern to **Jo Freeman's "The Tyranny of
  Structurelessness"** (1970/72) and treat "Princeps Problem" as our/Gemini's coinage, not an
  established term.
